# Cover Letter Rules

## Structure

- **Opening:** "I am submitting my application for [Position] at [Company]."
  - NOT "I am applying for...", "I am delighted to present...", "I humbly submit...", or corny variants
- **Salutation:** "Dear Hiring Committee," unless the JD names a specific person
- **Sign-off:** "Sincerely,\n\n[Full Name]" -- no hard breaks, no LaTeX `\\`

## Content

- **Research/stats framing:** Lead with the research question, then 2 methods with context, then map to the target role. Drop jargon.
  - Bad: listing method names (RNA-seq, ANOVA, mixed-effects) without context
  - Good: "My dissertation asked [question], tested across [scope]. Answering that required [method 1] to [purpose] and [method 2] to [purpose]."
- **"excited" count:** <=1 (closing paragraph preferred)
- **Statistical claims:** Same boundary as resume -- restrict to sections where you actually did the work
- **No AI tells:** tricolons, em/en dashes, rhetorical questions -- see `rules/ai_writing_tropes.md`
- **Req number:** include if available

## Length

- 250-350 words guideline (up to ~400 acceptable if it fits 1 page)
- Must fit on 1 page with 0.8in margins at 11pt

## Affiliation

- Keep employment affiliations consistent with resume but appropriate for the audience
- If you work through a staffing agency, you may omit the agency name in the CL while including it on the resume

## Cross-document consistency

- When fixing a factual claim in the resume, check the cover letter for the same claim
- Resume and CL should tell complementary stories, not identical ones
- Track both files in the same edit pass

## Additional Information / Free-text Fields

Some portals have free-text fields instead of CL uploads. Draft with the same standards: no tricolons, no em dashes, no AI tells.
